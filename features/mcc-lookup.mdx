The MCC Lookup tool lets you find the 4-digit Merchant Category Code (MCC) for any merchant or business type. Knowing the MCC of a transaction helps you predict which reward rate your credit card will apply — before you swipe.

## What are MCCs?

A **Merchant Category Code (MCC)** is a 4-digit number assigned by card networks (Visa, Mastercard, Amex, RuPay) to classify every business by the type of goods or services it sells. When you make a purchase, the merchant's MCC is included in the transaction data sent to your bank.

Banks use MCCs to:

- Apply **bonus reward rates** for preferred categories (e.g. 5x on dining, 3x on travel)
- **Exclude transactions** from earning rewards (e.g. rent, fuel surcharges, wallet loads)
- Calculate **transaction-level fees** such as foreign transaction or convenience charges

The same merchant can have a different MCC depending on whether you buy in-store vs. online, or whether they operate as a marketplace vs. a direct seller.

## How to use it

Type a merchant name, category keyword, or known merchant name in the search box. Results appear automatically after a short debounce delay — no need to press Enter.

Each result card shows:

- **MCC code** — the 4-digit code
- **Merchant name / category** — the official category description
- **Industry** — the broader industry group (e.g. Retail, Transportation, Amusement)
- **Category** — the sub-category within the industry
- **Similar merchants** — known merchants that typically fall under this MCC

<Tip>
  Copy the MCC code and paste it into the [Reward Calculator](/features/reward-calculator) to get an exact reward calculation for that category.
</Tip>

## How MCC affects reward rates

Most credit cards have a tiered reward structure based on MCCs:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Dining and restaurants">
    MCCs in the dining group (e.g. 5812 – Eating Places & Restaurants, 5814 – Fast Food Restaurants) commonly attract **bonus reward rates** on premium dining cards. Cards like HDFC Infinia and Amex Platinum charge offer elevated points for these codes.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Travel (airlines, hotels, taxis)">
    Airlines (MCC 3000–3299), hotels (MCC 3501–3999), car rentals (MCC 3351–3441), and ride-hailing apps fall into travel categories. Travel credit cards typically earn 2x–5x on these MCCs.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Grocery and supermarkets">
    Grocery stores (MCC 5411), supermarkets, and warehouse clubs (MCC 5300) are popular bonus categories on everyday cashback cards. Note that some large-format retailers may not code as groceries even if they sell groceries.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Fuel and petrol stations">
    Fuel stations (MCC 5541, 5542) often attract a fuel surcharge waiver benefit rather than bonus reward points. Check your card's terms — many cards exclude fuel from rewards or cap the benefit.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Online shopping and marketplaces">
    Online transactions may code under the merchant's own MCC rather than a generic online shopping code. For example, Amazon transactions may code as general merchandise (MCC 5999) or electronics (MCC 5732), depending on the seller.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Excluded categories">
    Several MCCs are commonly **excluded from earning rewards** across most Indian and Singapore cards:

    - Rent payments (MCC 6513)
    - Wallet loads and prepaid cards (MCC 6540)
    - Fuel surcharges (varies)
    - Government services (MCC 9311, 9399)
    - Insurance premiums (MCC 6300, 6311)

    Always check your card's most-excluded-categories list, as these can change.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Reporting missing or incorrect data

If you cannot find a merchant, or if the MCC shown appears incorrect, use the **Report Issue** button at the bottom of the page to submit a correction. Community reports help keep the database accurate for all users.
